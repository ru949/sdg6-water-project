Why SDG 6 Cannot Wait

Clean water is not a luxury. It is the foundation of health, education, and human dignity and billions are still without it.

Two Glasses. One Choice They Never Had.

In rural Ethiopia and Chad, contaminated water is not the worst option it is often the only one. Children drink what they can find, and the consequences are severe and preventable.

  • Diarrhoeal disease kills a child every 2 minutes
  • Contaminated water spreads cholera, typhoid, and dysentery
  • Safe water access could prevent 1.4 million deaths per year
See the Data

One Pipe. An Entire Village.

Where infrastructure exists at all, it is shared by hundreds. A single standpipe can mean a queue that begins before dawn and does not end until nightfall.

  • Over 40% of rural Africa relies on shared unprotected sources
  • Women and girls carry over 70% of the water-collection burden
  • School attendance drops sharply without on-site water access
Water & Communities

This Is What Change Looks Like.

When clean water arrives in a community, everything changes. Children go to school. Mothers reclaim hours of their day. This is achievable — and it starts with awareness.

  • Every $1 invested in water returns $4 in economic benefit
  • Girls' school enrolment rises by up to 15% with water on-site
  • Simple filtration can reduce child mortality by over 40%
